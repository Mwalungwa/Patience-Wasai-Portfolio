ABOUT ME

Hi, I am Patience Wasai , a dedicated Clinical Psychologist with a unique fusion of skills, blending a strong foundation in Human Resources with a deep passion for serving humanity. In addition to this I am an engaged and proactive member of the Kenya Counseling and Psychological Association

My Work

I currently serve as a Clinical Psychologist at the National Health Insurance Fund (NHIF), where my daily work has been instrumental in raising mental health awareness among the clients I support.

Beyond my professional endeavors, I am a devoted Christian and an active member of CITAM churches. I dedicate my time to mentoring young adults and actively engaging in counseling and advocacy within the faith community. I have also been a prominent figure in various training sessions and discussions, including my involvement in a Red Cross program focused on combating drug and substance abuse.

Competencies

In my role as a clinical psychologist, I am extensively involved in the assessment process, contributing to the formulation of diagnoses and providing psychological evaluations. This foundational step guides personalized treatment plans. Additionally, I administer a diverse range of psychological tests in hospital settings to measure disorders such as depression, anxiety, childhood learning disorders, and emotional issues.

Moving beyond assessment, I play a pivotal role in the treatment process. This involves providing psychotherapy, discussing patient progress with healthcare providers or family members, teaching coping skills, and educating individuals and families about specific mental health disorders. Furthermore, I engage in consultancy, collaborating with fellow professionals to share insights into psychological treatments and advocating for specific interventions when appropriate.

My commitment extends to teaching and training, participating in hospital-based practicums and academic activities like Continuous Medical Education (CMEs). In a research-oriented practicum, I actively contribute to clinical research under supervision, advancing our understanding of psychological processes. Lastly, I am deeply involved in community outreach, conducting psychotherapy in outreach programs, educating and training community members on mental health, and participating in initiatives like home visits and repatriation efforts. This comprehensive approach reflects the diverse competencies inherent in my role as a clinical psychologist.
